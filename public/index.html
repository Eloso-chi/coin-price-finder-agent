ℹ️ About This Site

What Is Coin Price Agent?

Coin Price Agent is a free research tool that helps coin collectors and dealers estimate the Fair Market Value (FMV) of U.S. coins and precious-metal bars. It aggregates real-time data from multiple industry sources—including recent sold-comp data, price guide references, and live precious-metal spot prices—and runs a proprietary valuation algorithm to produce a confidence-rated price estimate.

🔍 Confidence Score Key

Every valuation includes a confidence percentage (0–100%) that tells you how reliable the estimate is. The score is calculated from:

  • Sample size — how many comparable sold listings were found
  • Price consistency — how tightly the sold prices cluster together
  • Match quality — how closely comparable listings match your coin’s year, mint, and grade
  • PCGS verification — whether PCGS data corroborates the estimate
  • Data source — sold listings score higher than active (asking-price) listings
70–100% High confidence — strong data, reliable estimate
40–69% Moderate confidence — limited data, use as a guide
0–39% Low confidence — very few comps, treat as a rough estimate

🔒 Privacy & Security

Your coin collection data is never sent to our server. Here’s how it works:

  • All collection data is stored locally in your browser—it never leaves your device.
  • Every coin record is encrypted using industry-standard encryption before being written to storage. Only you hold the decryption key.
  • Encryption keys are derived from your password using a modern key-stretching algorithm—your password is never stored or transmitted.
  • The server has zero knowledge of your inventory. It cannot read, access, or reconstruct your collection.
  • If you clear your browser data, your encrypted collection is permanently deleted—we have no backup because we never had your data.

👤 How Login Works

The login system is client-only—no server accounts, no emails, no personal information.

  • When you create an account, a unique user ID and encryption key are derived from your username and password entirely in your browser using standard cryptographic methods.
  • A password verifier (a one-way hash) is stored in your browser so you can log back in, but your actual password is never stored anywhere.
  • No data is transmitted to the server during signup or login. The server does not know you have an account.
  • Your account exists only in this browser. If you switch browsers or devices, you’ll need to create a new account there.
  • Logging out clears the in-memory encryption key immediately. Your encrypted data remains in the browser but is unreadable without your password.
  • At signup you receive a one-time recovery phrase (8 words). Write it down—it’s the only way to recover your account if you forget your password.
